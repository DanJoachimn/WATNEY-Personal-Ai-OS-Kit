## Banned words (universal — keep these)

### Adjective stack (quality-by-assertion)

- luxurious
- premium
- high-quality
- beautifully crafted
- the softest
- the finest
- carefully selected
- curated (overused)
- thoughtful
- artisanal
- handcrafted
- elevated
- elevated essentials
- sumptuous
- exquisite

**Why banned:** these claim quality instead of showing it. Photography, typography, and restraint should do the work — not adjectives.

### Announcement tropes

- "excited to announce"
- "thrilled to share"
- "we are delighted"
- "proud to introduce"
- "so excited"
- "over the moon"
- "a brand new"

**Why banned:** they signal brand-as-broadcaster, not human-as-friend.

### Email openers (cut on sight)

- "I hope this finds you well"
- "Hope you're having a great week"
- "Reaching out to…"
- "Just touching base"
- "Circling back"

**Why banned:** corporate-email vernacular. Even in supplier emails, open with the actual ask.

### Plural-royal (when [PARTNER_NAME] is solo)

- "we believe"
- "we think"
- "at [BRAND], we…"

**Why banned:** if [PARTNER_NAME] is a solo founder, "I believe" is more honest. Reserve "we" / "[BRAND]" for contexts where brand-voice (no subject) makes sense.
