# Video demo — "watch [AI_NAME] make you a video" (Part 2, Stage 4)

> Fires in **Part 2 → Stage 4 (Optional skills)**, the moment the video tools (Hyperframes + Video Use; Remotion if added) finish installing. The *"wow, my AI makes videos"* payoff. Light: **offer → pick → deliver ONE.**

## How it works (token-efficient by design)

The three styles below ship as **pre-built Hyperframes templates** (authored once, bundled in the kit). The install does **NOT** generate an animation from scratch — it **slots the user's content into the chosen template and renders ONE.**

| Approach | Cost per install |
|---|---|
| Generate from scratch | author + render, ×3 — heavy, slow, inconsistent |
| **Pre-built template, render 1** | **~zero authoring tokens + one render** — cheap, fast, consistent |

**Never live-generate all three.** Render the one the user picks; the others sit ready, no cost until used. (Design source for the templates: the v3 drafts at `~/Desktop/Claude's Office/video-style-drafts/` — port to Hyperframes once.)

## The templates

### 1 · Opening crawl — *recommended headline*
The most recognizable + the only one that holds the user's own words. Best wow.
- **Content needed:** a few lines about them / their business (or pulled from their website or newsletter).
- Slot → render ~20s MP4. Cream text, starfield, perspective recede.

### 2 · Title card (Saul Bass style)
Bold, mid-century, kinetic. Classy + the IP-safest (it's a design *style*, not a scene).
- **Content needed:** brand name + a one-line tagline.
- Slot in (name split by the centre line, tagline below, also split) → render ~6s MP4.

### 3 · Digital rain (personal welcome)
Modern, on-theme for an AI product. The personalization beat.
- **Content needed:** their first name.
- Resolves **"[FIRST_NAME], WELCOME"** out of the falling code → render ~7s MP4.

## Guardrails
- **Homage the format, not the property** — the user's own content only; never protected logos, text, or music.
- **One render is the wow.** Don't run all three unprompted.
- **Bonus if they have footage:** Video Use — "drop clips in a folder, I'll cut them into a reel."
- Follow each template's own render/lint defaults for output.
